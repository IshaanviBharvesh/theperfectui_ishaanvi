Manali,Himachal Pradesh TOP

A gift of the Himalayas to the world, Manali is a beautiful township nestled in the picturesque Beas River valley. It is a rustic enclave known for its cool climate and snow-capped mountains, offering respite to tourists escaping scorching heat of the plains.

What is Manali also known as? The particular spot where he established his abode was the present Manali which is regarded as the changed name of 'Manu-Alaya', the abode of Manu. The temple dedicated to Manu is still existing in the Manali village.

Dalhousie, Himachal Pradesh TOP

A wonderful retreat in the lap of the stunning Himalayas, Dalhousie is known for snow-capped mountains, pine-clad valleys, gorgeous landscapes and gurgling waterfalls. The valley also boasts a rich colonial past as it was a popular summer destination among the British.

In fact, you can still come across beautiful Victorian-era architecture here. Khajjiar, a small and beautiful town, 20 km from Dalhousie, is a must-visit too.

Mussoorie, Uttarakhand TOP

Located 34 km from Dehradun, Mussoorie is a picturesque hill station that promises a pleasant climate and abundant natural beauty. The town is surrounded by Shivalik Ranges and Doon Valley and is one of the most beautiful places to visit in India.

Mussoorie, which is popularly known as the Queen of the Hills, was once a popular summer retreat for the British. And the quaint churches and hotels here are a wonderful reminder of Mussoorie’s colonial past.

Nainital, Uttarakhand TOP

Situated at an elevation of 2084 m above sea level, Nainital is a beautiful hill resort in Uttarakhand’s Kumaon region. Thanks to its proximity to Dehradun and Delhi, this hill station is one of the most frequented places in North India.

Boasting scenic landscapes, pleasant weather and a peaceful environment, Nainital plays the perfect host to those looking to spend time amidst nature, away from the scorching heat of plains.

From boating on Naini Lake to paying your respects at Naina Devi Temple, there are plenty of things to do in this quaint little hill station. Nainital has many vantage points and trekking trails as well.

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh TOP

Shimla hill station is still the colonial summer capital (resort) to which British used to retreat in when heat of Indio-Gangetic plains used to become unbearable.

Be it grey-stone work of Vice Regal Lodge, or Gaiety Theater putting neo-gothic architectural excellence, the beauty of building seem to come alive and not only staying in eyes of beholder.

The Elizabethan style mixed and merged with different other forms in buildings like Ellerslie, Christ Church, Gorton Castle, Wood Ville, etc are a must-see.
